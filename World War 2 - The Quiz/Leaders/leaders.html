    Allies

    Winston Churchill (Great Britain)


    Sir Winston Leonard Spencer Churchill was a British statesman who served as Prime Minister of the United Kingdom from 1940 to 1945 (during the second world war). Churchill was born on 30 November 1874 at his family's ancestral home, Blenheim Palace in Oxfordshire, England. His father, Lord Randolph Churchill, served as an MP for Woodstock; his mother, Jennie Spencer Churchill, was a daughter of Leonard Jerome, a wealthy American businessman.


    Churchill was a member of the Liberal Party from 1904 to 1924, before joining the Conservatives the following year. He is widely considered to be one of the most prominent figures of the 20th century, and one of the most important leaders in the history of Great Britain.






    Franklin D. Roosevelt (United States)


    Franklin Delano Roosevelt (FDR) was an American politician and attorney who served as the 32nd president of the United States from 1933 until his death in 1945. Roosevelt was born on January 30, 1882, in Hyde Park, New York, to businessman James Roosevelt and his second wife, Sara Ann Delano. They were sixth cousins and both came from wealthy New York families.






    Joseph Stalin (USSR)


    Joseph Vissarionovich Stalin was a Georgian revolutionary and the second leader of the Soviet Union (from 1922 until his death in 1953).
